Research

MBIS Lab develops multimodal biomedical imaging systems, mobile healthcare devices, and AI algorithms that turn raw sensor data into clinically and geospatially actionable information.

Our work spans four directions: multimodal biomedical imaging systems, mobile and connected healthcare, deep learning for biomedical image analysis and enhancement, and AI for remote sensing. Across all four, we build the sensing hardware and the learning algorithms together — from high-frequency ultrasound and multispectral optics to domain-adaptive and test-time-adaptive neural networks — and validate them in real clinical and industrial settings.

Research Areas

Multimodal Biomedical System Optical, ultrasound, and multimodal biomedical imaging systems

Development of multimodal imaging systems that combine complementary contrast mechanisms — optical, spectral, and acoustic — for early detection and characterization of disease. Our systems integrate multispectral and photometric-stereo endoscopy, high-frequency acoustic radiation force impulse (ARFI) biomicroscopy, and single-beam acoustic tweezers for probing cell mechanics. Recent work extends into acoustic holography and ultrasound-based tissue optical clearing.

Mobile & Connected Healthcare System Smartphone-based diagnostics and privacy-preserving learning

Development of portable and smartphone-based diagnostic systems, together with the privacy-preserving learning frameworks needed to deploy them at scale. Our smartphone multispectral imaging platform quantitatively discriminates skin lesions such as seborrheic dermatitis and psoriasis, and our federated learning work enables multi-institution model training on thyroid ultrasound and personalized skin diagnosis without sharing patient data. We also develop deep learning-based ECG classification and smartphone-based indoor localization for connected care environments.

Deep Learning-based Image Analysis and Enhancement AI-driven medical image analysis, segmentation, and enhancement

Development of deep learning architectures for medical image segmentation, enhancement, and diagnosis, with an emphasis on precise boundary delineation and adaptation to unseen clinical domains. Our contributions include the USIM Gate upsampling operator (AISTATS 2023), stochastic adaptive activation functions (NeurIPS 2022), and shape-memory networks for contextual semantic interpretation (ICLR 2025). Clinically, these methods power expert-level diagnosis of incomplete Kawasaki disease from echocardiography, breast tumor segmentation under speckle noise, real-time ultrasound enhancement for rotator cuff tear diagnosis, and velopharyngeal insufficiency assessment from videofluoroscopy.

AI for Remote Sensing Deep learning for aerial and satellite image analysis

Development of deep learning algorithms for object extraction and change detection in high-resolution aerial and satellite imagery, in collaboration with Dabeeo Inc., a geospatial data company. A central challenge in remote sensing is the domain gap: a model trained on one city, season, or sensor degrades sharply on another. Our research addresses this through domain-adaptive and test-time-adaptive learning — including self-mutating networks (ICCV 2021), transfer-attack-based domain adaptation, and path-selective test-time adaptation — while also targeting the fine-grained boundary accuracy required for automated digital map generation. Applications extend to urban land change monitoring, defense, and autonomous driving.

Projects

Multimodal Endoscopy System Optical-multispectral and high-frequency-ultrasound endoscopy for gastric tumor detection

A forward-looking endoscopic system fusing optical multispectral and high-frequency ultrasound imaging for real-time gastric tumor detection (IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging, 2020), later extended with photometric-stereo imaging for 3D lesion topography to aid delineation of malignant gastric lesions (Biomedical Optics Express, 2019).

endoscopy multimodal-imaging multispectral-imaging high-frequency-ultrasound gastric-lesion
Mobile Multispectral Imaging for Skin Care Deep learning-based discrimination of scalp skin lesions via smartphone multispectral imaging

Smartphone-based multispectral imaging with machine learning-based discrimination between seborrheic dermatitis and psoriasis on the scalp (Biomedical Optics Express, 2019), extended with mSpecFusion-Net, a multimodal deep fusion network for differential diagnosis of psoriasis and dermatitis (MICCAI 2026).

mobile-healthcare multispectral-imaging smartphone-imaging skin-diagnosis deep-learning
Deep Learning for Ultrasound Denoising and Wearable Monitoring Self-attention denoising and coarse-to-fine shape estimation for wearable ultrasound

A multi-scale self-attention network for denoising medical ultrasound images (APSIPA Transactions on Signal and Information Processing, 2024), and a deep learning-based coarse-to-fine shape estimation model for accurate bladder volume monitoring on wearable ultrasound devices (IEEE Transactions on Ultrasonics, Ferroelectrics, and Frequency Control, 2024).

deep-learning image-enhancement ultrasound wearable-ultrasound
Ultrasound Neuromodulation System Acoustic hologram techniques for transcranial ultrasound neuromodulation

A machine learning-enhanced, skull-universal acoustic hologram for efficient transcranial ultrasound neuromodulation across varied rodent skulls (IEEE Transactions on Ultrasonics, Ferroelectrics, and Frequency Control, 2024), extended to a physics-aware hologram technique for high-fidelity multi-focal stimulation (Brain Stimulation, 2026).

ultrasound neuromodulation acoustic-hologram biomedical-system
Boundary-Precise Building Extraction from Aerial Imagery Boundary-oriented segmentation for manual-delineation-level digital mapping

A boundary-oriented binary building segmentation model with two-scheme learning for aerial images (IEEE Transactions on Geoscience & Remote Sensing, 2021), developed with Dabeeo Inc. to reach manual-delineation-level footprint accuracy for automated digital map generation.

Federated Learning for Privacy-Preserving Medical AI Multi-institutional model training validated in real healthcare environments

Federated learning frameworks validated in real clinical settings: multi-institution thyroid ultrasound analysis that protects patient information (JMIR Medical Informatics), and a fine-tuning network for personalized skin diagnosis across institutions (MICCAI 2023).

federated-learning privacy-preserving-ai ultrasound skin-diagnosis
High-frequency Ultrasound Microbeam Technique Non-contact acoustic techniques for probing cell mechanics

Non-contact high-frequency ultrasound microbeam and single-beam acoustic tweezer techniques for probing cell mechanics, applied to mechanotransduction in vascular endothelial cells (Ultrasound in Medicine & Biology, 2014) and intracellular calcium signaling in breast cancer cells (Ultrasonics, 2015).
